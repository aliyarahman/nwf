Surge in Zero-Deforestation Commitments

In the past several years, there has been a surge in zero-deforestation commitments. In 2010, the Consumer Goods Forum (CGF), a global industry network of more than 400 retailers, manufacturers, service providers, and associations, approved a board resolution, pledging to mobilize resources to achieve zero net deforestation by 2020.27 The CGF aims to achieve this through the responsible sourcing of key agricultural commodities, including beef. In support for this commitment, the CGF created the Tropical Forest Alliance 2020 (TFA), to help address deforestation and the sourcing of beef and other key commodities, bringing together industry, national governments and civil society groups in the largest public-private initiative of this type.

In 2014, governments, multi-national companies, and civil society groups came together at the United Nations Climate Summit to sign the New York Declaration on Forests, a pledge to support public and private sector goals aimed at eliminating deforestation from the production of agricultural commodities, including beef. A few of the key target outcomes include:28

  • Cut the rate of global deforestation in half by 2020 and strive to end forest loss by 2030.
  • Restore 150 million hectares of degraded landscapes by 2020 and 200 million hectares by 2030.
  • Strengthen forest governance and transparency
  • Reward countries and jurisdictions that, by taking action, reducing forest emissions – particularly through public policy and private sector sourcing.

Achieving these (and other) target outcomes could reduce emissions by 4.5 – 8.8 billion tons per year by 2030.

In addition, a myriad of retailers and manufacturers have stepped up, individually, to make commitments to zero-deforestation, including prominent global brands, such as Mars29, Colgate-Palmolive30, Walmart31, 32, Tesco33, and many more. Commitments to zero-deforestation production are helping support positive solution-oriented improvements throughout the value chain. The implementation of these commitments will drive on-the-ground transformation and help ensure that beef, leather, and tallow can continue to be produced in the Brazilian Amazon without jeopardizing tropical forests, and other valuable ecosystems.
